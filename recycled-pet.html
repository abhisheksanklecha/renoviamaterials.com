Recycling routes
Mechanical

Super-clean PCR PET

Post-consumer bottles are sorted, washed, shredded to flake and re-extruded into pellets — keeping the polymer chain intact. A super-clean decontamination step removes migrants, and these processes hold FDA Letters of No Objection and EFSA approvals, making mechanical rPET the established, lowest-energy route for food-contact bottle-to-bottle recycling.

Each cycle causes a small IV drop and a slight rise in yellowness (higher b*, lower L*), corrected via solid-state IV build and light colour adjustment.

Best for: bottles, sheet, fibre and virgin blends — food & non-food.
Chemical

Monomer-grade depolymerised PET

Depolymerisation breaks PET back to its building blocks — BHET, DMT or purified PTA + MEG depending on the route (glycolysis, methanolysis or hydrolysis) — which are purified and re-polymerised into new PET.

Because the recycle history is erased at the monomer level, the output is virgin-equivalent on IV, colour, AA and DEG, and can take coloured or mixed feedstock. EFSA and FDA recognise these routes for food contact; Renovia sources from audited partners with full mass-balance documentation.

Best for: clear food & pharma packaging at 100% PCR with no optical trade-off.

FAQ
What PCR percentages are available?

Standard grades at 30%, 50%, 75% and 100% post-consumer content. FDA LNO and EFSA approvals are available up to 100% PCR for food contact.

Does rPET perform differently from virgin?

Grades are engineered to virgin-equivalent IV, colour and processing. At very high PCR a slight yellowness (b*) shift can appear, managed with colour correction or by blending with virgin.

Where does the feedstock come from?

Certified collection streams — primarily deposit-return and curbside. Every batch carries origin, processing history and GRS chain-of-custody documentation.

Can I use rPET for carbonated drinks?

Yes. Bottle grade (IV 0.80–0.84) is built for CSD with strong CO₂ retention and pressure resistance, including 100% recycled-content bottles.
